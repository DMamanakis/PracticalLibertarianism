I’m curious whether ranked-choice voting would make more people willing to participate in elections.

Under ranked-choice voting, you could rank candidates in order of preference.

For example:

1. Green Party
2. Democratic Socialists
3. Democratic Party

Or:

1. Libertarian
2. Republican
3. Independent

Your first-choice vote would stay with that candidate unless the candidate was eliminated. Only then would your vote transfer to your next choice. You would not be required to rank anyone you did not support.

The idea is that you could vote for the candidate you truly prefer without being told:

“Your vote is wasted.”

“You’re splitting the vote.”

“You’re helping the candidate you dislike most.”

Would ranked-choice voting make you more likely to vote?

**Poll options:**

* Yes, I would be more likely to vote.
* I already vote, but I could vote more honestly.
* No, it would not affect whether I vote.
* No, I would be less likely to vote.
* I need to understand ranked-choice voting better.

I’m especially interested in hearing from people who sometimes do not vote because neither major-party candidate represents them.

This is not a scientific poll. I’m trying to understand whether allowing voters to rank their sincere first choice and an acceptable fallback might increase participation and reduce the feeling that third-party votes do not count.

Please explain your answer in the comments.
