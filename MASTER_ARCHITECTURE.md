# Master Architecture Document

## Purpose

Define the governing architecture, scope, and quality standards for the
Practical Libertarianism project.

## Mission

Produce a constitutionally grounded manifesto proposing practical
reforms that preserve liberty while maintaining effective government.

## Audience

-   General readers
-   Policymakers
-   Legislators
-   Constitutional scholars
-   Students

## Design Principles

1.  Preserve constitutional institutions whenever possible.
2.  Push authority to the lowest practical level.
3.  Separate current law from proposed reform.
4.  Every factual claim should be traceable to evidence.
5.  Distinguish clearly between:
    -   Current law
    -   Historical analysis
    -   Opinion
    -   Recommendation

## Repository Layers

### Manifesto Chapters

Public-facing manuscript.

### Research Papers

Deep supporting analysis and source material.

### Proposals

Working drafts including: - Constitutional amendments - Model
legislation - Implementation plans - Election models - Mathematical
analysis - White papers

## Chapter Standard

Every chapter should contain:

1.  Executive Summary
2.  Current Constitutional Framework
3.  Historical Context
4.  Current Law
5.  Problem Statement
6.  Proposed Solution
7.  Advantages
8.  Risks / Counterarguments
9.  Implementation
10. Conclusion
11. References

## Quality Gates

Research → Outline → Draft → Constitutional Review → Technical Review →
Citation Review → Editorial Review → Publish

## Versioning

Semantic versioning is recommended:

Major.Minor.Revision

Example:

1.4.2

## Long-Term Goal

Produce a publishable book supported by independently verifiable
research and companion proposal documents suitable for legislative
discussion.
